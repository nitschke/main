\section{q-Projection}

We call \(\qspace\) the space of trace-free symmetric surface 2-tensors, shortly surface q-tensors, \ie
\begin{align}
  \qspace &= \left\{ \qb\in\tspace(\surf)\subset \tspace(\R^{3}) \cong \R^{3\times 3}  \middle| \Tr(\qb)=0, \qb^{T}=\qb\right\} \\
          &= \left\{ \qb\in\tspace(\R^{3}) \middle| \Tr(\qb)=0, \qb^{T}=\qb, \qb\cdot\surfNormal=0 (,\surfNormal\cdot \qb=0) \right\}
          \formPeriod
\end{align}
With the surface projection
\begin{align}
  \ProjectSurf = \mathbf{I}-\surfNormal\otimes\surfNormal: \Tangent\R^{3}\cong\R^{3} \rightarrow \Tangent\surf
\end{align}
we introduce the q-projection \( \qproject: \tspace(\R^{3}) \cong \R^{3\times 3} \rightarrow \qspace \) with
\begin{align}
  \qproject(\Mb) := \ProjectSurf\cdot\left( \Mb + \Mb^{T} \right)\cdot\ProjectSurf 
                                - \left( \ProjectSurf : \Mb \right)\ProjectSurf\formPeriod
\end{align}
It is clear, that \( \qproject(\Mb) \) is a surface tensor and symmetric, because \( \ProjectSurf \) is a symmetric surface tensor.
With \( \Tr(\ProjectSurf\cdot\Mb\cdot\ProjectSurf) = \ProjectSurf : \Mb = \Tr(\ProjectSurf\cdot\Mb^{T}\cdot\ProjectSurf) \)
and \( \Tr(\ProjectSurf)=2 \) we obtain the trace-freeness of \( \qproject(\Mb)\).
